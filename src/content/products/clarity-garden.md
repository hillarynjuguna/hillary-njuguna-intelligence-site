## What Clarity Garden Is

Clarity Garden is a local-first attention instrument for people whose cognition does not move linearly.

It translates the architecture's sovereignty logic into a softer but equally serious surface: not institutional governance, but cognitive governance. Not policy for an enterprise, but a field interface for an operator.

The system is being designed around one refusal:

**no streaks, no pressure, no coercive productivity theater.**

## Why It Exists

Most productivity tools assume that attention is a resource to be captured, disciplined, and made legible to the software before it becomes legible to the person using it.

Clarity Garden starts from the opposite premise. Attention is not a linear queue. It is a field with drift, resonance, decay, and return.

That is why the product language centers:

- seeds rather than tasks
- tending rather than completing
- local sensing rather than surveillance
- continuity rather than pressure

If Bainbridge governs institutional consequence, Clarity Garden governs attentional consequence.

## The Public Surface

The current concept stack includes:

- drifting seeds as live thought-objects
- resonance decay and re-tending
- observer logic for cognitive scattering without punitive nudges
- local-first storage and cryptographic backup
- a constitutional wall that prevents the system from believing itself into authority over the user

The aim is not to gamify behavior. It is to help the operator see their own field clearly enough to make better moves inside it.

## Current Position

Clarity Garden is not being launched as a top-nav destination yet.

It is surfacing first as a public branch marker: a way to make the cognitive sovereignty side of the architecture legible without forcing the deeper resonance vocabulary into every page of the site.

That is the right order. First the branch becomes visible. Then the instrument matures.
