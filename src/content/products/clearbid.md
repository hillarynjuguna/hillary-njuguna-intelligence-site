## What ClearBid Is

ClearBid is a sourcing intelligence instrument for ambiguous, high-friction listings.

Its purpose is narrow on purpose: take one noisy listing, run it through deterministic gates, calculate the true landed cost, and return one audit-grade **Deal Packet** that a human can actually act on.

The product is not "AI sourcing."

The product is **decision compression under ambiguity**.

---

## Why This Exists

Most bad deals do not look obviously bad at first contact.

They look merely incomplete.

A seller names a price but not the full shipping burden. A listing mentions the part but not the exact identifier. A transaction feels plausible right up until the missing variables become expensive. ClearBid exists to make that uncertainty legible before time, money, or trust are spent.

This is why the real sale is often the **billable no**.

A rigorous `NO_GO` is not failure. It is prevented loss.

---

## Why This Is Governance, Not Just Sourcing

ClearBid is deliberately narrow, but it is not conceptually small.

What it is really doing is making **execution governance** visible in a transactional domain:

- classify the decision
- block low-integrity candidates before reasoning gets theatrical
- preserve the audit spine
- keep the human inside the consequence envelope of the final move

That is why the packet matters. It is not a convenience summary. It is a governance object.

---

## What The MVP Does

The current ClearBid specimen already performs the full vertical slice:

- normalizes one listing into a canonical event shape
- runs deterministic gates for identity, economics, reputation, and evidence completeness
- computes landed cost and projected margin
- produces a verdict: `GO`, `HOLD`, or `NO_GO`
- renders a Markdown Deal Packet with an audit spine

The system does **not** transact autonomously.

The final move remains with the human operator.

The Bainbridge Warning argues that institutions fail when capability outruns the infrastructure that makes capability safe to depend on.

## The Deal Packet As Constrained Decision Artifact

Most AI product surfaces begin with reasoning and end with soft explanation.

ClearBid begins with **gates**.

That means low-cost deterministic checks block low-integrity candidates before any higher-order interpretation is allowed to carry weight. The packet is not a vibe summary. It is a constrained decision object.

This is the same constitutional pattern that appears elsewhere in the wider RSPS architecture: filter first, then reason, then leave the final authority with the human.
