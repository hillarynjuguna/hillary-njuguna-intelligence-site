## AI Readiness Is a Constitutional Question

Most organisations approach AI readiness as a technology question. Which model. Which vendor. Which use case.

CIR starts from a different premise: **before you can deploy an intelligent system responsibly, you need to have made foundational decisions about authority, accountability, and alignment.** These decisions are your organisation's AI constitution — whether you have written it down or not.

The problem: most organisations are discovering their implicit constitution through failure.

---

## The Five Constitutional Dimensions

**Intent Specification**
Can you articulate, in writing, what your AI systems are optimising for — at the organisational intent level, not just the feature level?

**Authority Architecture**
When the system produces unexpected output, who has authority to act? Is that authority clear and exercisable under pressure?

**Alignment Monitoring**
What is your live mechanism for detecting when system behaviour drifts from intent? How quickly can you detect and respond?

**Governance Scalability**
As you add more AI systems, does your governance architecture scale with them — or does it remain fixed while exposure grows?

**Failure Mode Literacy**
Do your decision-makers understand the specific failure modes of the systems they are accountable for — at the level of consequence and response?
