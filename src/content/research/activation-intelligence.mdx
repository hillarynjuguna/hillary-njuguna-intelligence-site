Intelligence, in its most potent form, is not a static pool of knowledge but a dynamic resonance that only becomes visible upon **Activation**. 

While large language models (LLMs) provide the latent potential (the 'Reasoning Substrate'), **Activation Intelligence** is what bridges the gap between potentiality and utility. It is the mechanism through which a system identifies an environmental trigger—a WhatsApp message, a pricing shift, a governance breach—and converts it into an **Active Payload**.

### The Calibration Layer (Mythos Integration)

Recent architectural insights into "Calibration Layers" (cf. Mythos Framework) suggest that raw activation is insufficient for sovereign systems. A system that activates without calibration is a liability.

A **Calibration Layer** serves as a real-time oversight mechanism that intersects the activation path. It asks:
1.  **Alignment**: Is this payload consistent with the node's long-term objectives?
2.  **Safety**: Does this action violate any `R0-R3` reversibility constraints?
3.  **Accuracy**: Is the generated response calibrated to the specific relational context of the requester?

### From Event to Payloads

In the **Activation Intelligence Engine**, the flow follows a strictly defined path:

1.  **Ingestion**: Capture of the environmental signal (Substrate Agnostic).
2.  **Calibration**: Contextual filtering through the node's current governance state.
3.  **Synthesis**: Generation of the specific, high-intent payload.
4.  **Execution**: Writeback to the physical/business substrate (e.g., LHDN E-Invoicing injection).

By formalizing this sequence, we move from "chat-based AI" to **Agentic Sovereignty**, where every activation is high-intent and governed by a local-first calibration protocol.
