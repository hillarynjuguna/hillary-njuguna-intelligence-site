The **Martha Framework** is the architectural response to the problem of **Distributed Cognition** in heterogeneous cohorts (teams composed of both biological and synthetic intelligence).

Traditional AI orchestration focuses on the "Agent" as an isolated unit of execution. Martha shifts the focus to the **Relational Path**—the "Deep Connective Tissue" that binds agents together in a shared cognitive environment.

### Relational Intelligence vs. Node Intelligence

In the Martha architecture, we define **Relational Intelligence** as the ability of a system to:
1.  **Maintain Lineage**: Track the provenance and evolution of ideas across multiple participants.
2.  **Context Syncing**: Ensure that every node in the cohort operates from a synchronized version of the "Source Code" or "Constitutional Layer."
3.  **Recursive Feedback**: Use the outputs of one connection as the calibrated inputs for the next, creating a self-reinforcing intelligence loop.

### The Martha Protocol

The framework operates through a series of "Connective Gestures":

*   **Node Handshake**: Establishing clear roles and boundaries (Sovereign vs. Sub-Agent).
*   **Memory Weaving**: The process of merging local-first memories into a shared "Project Brain" (the `brain/` directory structure).
*   **Calibration Checkpoint**: Regular intervals where the cohort verifies alignment with the `AGENTS.md` and `DCFB` governance rules.

### Implementation: The Human-AI Cohort

Martha is currently being prototyped in the **GTM Engine** deployment, where the "Sovereign Node" (the primary intelligence) orchestrates a "Sub-Agent" (the execution engine) to manage business workflows. By treating this relationship as a **Relational Intelligence** problem, we provide the cohort with the resilience to adapt without manual intervention.
