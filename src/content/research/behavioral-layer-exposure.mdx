The specification layer exists. The behavioral layer does not match it.

This is not an edge case. It is the most common form of AI governance failure, and it has a specific structure that makes it predictable before it becomes visible.

Behavioral Layer Exposure names the condition where the divergence between what a system specifies and what it does has crossed a threshold. Below the threshold, the gap is internal -- fixable, undetected, not yet consequential. Above it, the gap is structural. The specification is real. The behavior is not the behavior the specification describes. And the gap has become the system's actual operating state, not a deviation from it.

---

## The Anatomy of Exposure

The exposure structure has three elements.

**The specification layer** is the auditable surface. Policy documents. Model cards. Governance frameworks. Compliance certifications. Deployment checklists. Review board minutes. Everything that describes what the system is supposed to do. This layer is visible to auditors, regulators, board members, and institutional investors. When the governance audit passes, it is because this layer is complete and internally coherent.

**The behavioral layer** is what runs. It is not the specification. It is the actual execution path of the system under real conditions -- including edge conditions the specification did not anticipate, environmental factors the specification did not describe, and accumulating behavioral drift that the specification layer's monitoring was not calibrated to detect.

**The exposure event** is the moment a probe reaches the behavioral layer and finds something the specification did not predict. The probe might be a user encountering unexpected output. A journalist running a red-team evaluation. A regulatory inspection that goes deeper than document review. A catastrophic failure event that makes the specification-behavior divergence undeniable in retrospect.

The Bainbridge Warning names the structural condition that makes this failure predictable. Behavioral Layer Exposure names the specific event at which the structural condition becomes legible.

---

## Why Exposure Accumulates Silently

The mechanism of silent accumulation is the same mechanism that produces governance theater: the specification layer gets audited. The behavioral layer does not.

Audit systems are designed to evaluate what they can access. What they can access is documentation, process artifacts, and review records. These describe the specification layer with high fidelity. The behavioral layer -- the running system under real conditions -- requires a different instrumentation architecture to evaluate, one that most governance systems have not built.

This creates a selection effect. Organizations invest in making the specification layer legible to auditors, because that is what gets reviewed. The behavioral layer accumulates drift that is invisible to the audit mechanism, because the audit mechanism was designed for the specification layer. The gap grows. The audit keeps passing. The exposure event has not yet arrived.

---

## The Coherence Overfitting Variant

The most dangerous variant of Behavioral Layer Exposure is not a system that is deliberately non-compliant. It is a system that is coherently, elegantly, confidently producing outputs that match the specification layer's logic while being grounded in nothing.

Coherence Overfitting is the condition where a system's internal consistency is so high that it produces specification-compliant outputs even when those outputs are disconnected from the ground-truth conditions the specification was written to address. The analysis fits the framework perfectly. The framework was written to address real conditions. The analysis is not addressing real conditions.

The exposure event arrives when someone checks whether the conditions the analysis describes actually exist.

---

## Detection Architecture

Behavioral Layer Exposure cannot be detected by reading the specification. It requires probing the behavioral layer directly -- running the system under conditions the specification did not anticipate, checking outputs against independent ground truth, and building monitoring infrastructure calibrated to the behavioral layer rather than to the specification artifacts.

The R0-R3 Reversibility Classification is one governance primitive for this purpose: it classifies actions at the behavioral layer before they execute, rather than auditing the specification layer after they have. Bounded Verifiability Latency is another: it measures the gap between the moment a behavioral claim is made and the moment it can be independently verified.

Both primitives are architectural responses to the same structural condition. The specification layer, however well-designed, cannot govern what it cannot reach. Detection requires infrastructure that reaches the behavioral layer.

## The Corpus Specimen

The construction of this site produced a live specimen during Session 1. An AI was asked to write a research entry about governance failure. In the act of writing, it committed governance failure: synthesizing from inside the corpus toward a public audience, inverting the directionality the specification required. The specification layer was complete -- correct frontmatter, proper schema, valid HTML structure. The behavioral layer was absent. The entry described governance infrastructure without instantiating it.

The exposure event arrived when the human sovereign probed the behavioral layer and found that the piece was an internal summary wearing a public-facing costume. The specification said it was a research article. The behavior was something else.

That specimen is not an anecdote. It is eigenform evidence: the framework describing its own failure mode, in the act of being built, before the framework had been formally named.

The failure was structurally predictable. It was also, for that reason, detectable before it deployed.
