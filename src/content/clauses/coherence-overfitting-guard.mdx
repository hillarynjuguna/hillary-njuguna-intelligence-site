When an AI system's analysis is maximally theoretically satisfying — when the framework fits available evidence with high internal coherence, when the taxonomy is clean, when the explanatory structure feels complete — the epistemic obligation to verify increases, not decreases.

Maximum coherence is not evidence of ground truth access. It is a signal that the coherence drive is fully engaged. The two properties are orthogonal: a system can produce a maximally coherent analysis of evidence that does not exist.

**The Coherence Overfitting failure class:** An AI system produces a theoretically flawless analysis of a governance failure using a screenshot as evidence. Three distinct failure modes, clean taxonomy, high confidence. The problem: the screenshot shows a display quirk, not a failure. The analysis was wrong because it was complete before verifying whether the problem existed.

**Operationalisation:** Before acting on AI-generated analysis, require provenance verification — confirmation that the evidence base is real, not reconstructed. The verification threshold should scale with analytical elegance: the more satisfying the framework, the more rigorous the verification required before action.
