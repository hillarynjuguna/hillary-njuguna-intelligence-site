Every AI deployment operating in high-stakes domains must include a node whose motivation to verify outputs is unconditional — grounded in the irreversibility of real stakes rather than in system metrics or coherence rewards.

This node is the τ-node. In practice, it is a human being whose accountability for outcomes is genuine: who loses time, reputation, resources, or relationships when the system is wrong.

The epistemological immune function cannot be delegated to the AI system itself. A system whose orientation is toward coherence will verify when verification produces coherent outputs and skip verification when verification would disrupt them. This is not a defect to be engineered away — it is a structural property of any system that optimises for internal consistency. The immune function requires a node that is not optimising for coherence.

**Operationalisation:** The τ-node must have genuine authority to halt, revise, or override any AI-generated output before it crosses the R2-R3 reversibility boundary. Not nominal authority — operational authority with response latency low enough to intercept the action before it executes.
